Sisig

Description:

Sisig is a Filipino dish renowned for its savory, spicy, and often tangy flavors. It typically consists of chopped and grilled pig's face parts, such as ears, snout, and cheeks, seasoned with calamansi (Filipino lime), vinegar, soy sauce, and various spices. Sisig is often served on a sizzling hot plate, creating a crispy texture on the edges and a rich, flavorful experience. Variations of sisig may include other meats like chicken or fish for those who prefer alternatives to pork.

Ingredients:

  • Pork Face Parts
  • Onions
  • Calamansi
  • Soy Sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Garlic
  • Butter

Steps:

  1. To prepare sisig, start by boiling and grilling pork face parts like ears, snout, and cheeks until they achieve a crispy texture. Chop the grilled pork into fine pieces. In a pan, saut√© finely chopped onions and minced garlic until fragrant.

  2. Add the chopped pork to the pan and continue cooking until it develops a golden brown color. Season the mixture with calamansi or lemon juice, soy sauce, vinegar, and finely chopped red and green chili peppers according to your desired level of spiciness. Optionally, incorporate mashed chicken liver for added richness.

  3. Finish by seasoning with salt and pepper to taste. If preferred, mix in mayonnaise for a creamy texture. Serve the sisig on a sizzling hot plate for a delightful and flavorful experience.
