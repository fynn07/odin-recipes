Humba

Description:

Humba is a Filipino dish known for its sweet and savory flavor profile, featuring pork belly or hocks braised in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and sugar until tender. It often incorporates ingredients like dried banana blossoms and black beans, contributing to its unique taste.

Ingredients:

  • Pork Belly or Hocks
  • Soy Sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Garlic
  • Dried Banana Blossoms
  • Black beans
  • Bay Leaves
  • Oil
  • Water

Steps:

  1. To make humba, start by cutting pork belly or hocks into serving pieces. Marinate the pork in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, brown sugar, minced garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Saut√© the marinated pork in oil until browned, then add the remaining marinade and enough water to cover the meat. Bring it to a boil, reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours until the pork is tender, stirring occasionally.

  3. Add rehydrated dried banana blossoms and optional black beans, continuing to simmer until the sauce thickens. Adjust the seasoning, and serve the humba hot over steamed rice.
