Sinigang

Description:

Sinigang is a Filipino sour soup known for its distinctive tangy flavor, typically achieved through the use of tamarind as a souring agent. It features a variety of ingredients such as pork, shrimp, or beef, along with a mix of vegetables like water spinach, radish, and eggplant, creating a comforting and flavorful dish.

Ingredients:

  • Meat
  • Water
  • Radish
  • String Beans
  • Leafy Vegetables
  • Green Chili Peppers
  • Fish Sauce
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Onions

Steps:

  1. To prepare Sinigang, start by boiling about 8 cups of water in a pot, then add your chosen protein—whether it's pork ribs, pork belly, beef shank, or shrimp—and let it simmer until the meat is partially cooked.

  2. Introduce sliced radish and green beans, allowing them to soften, followed by eggplant, leafy vegetables like kangkong or spinach, and green chili peppers if desired. Dissolve 2-3 tablespoons of tamarind paste or use a tamarind Sinigang mix in the broth, season with 2-3 tablespoons of fish sauce (Patis), and add salt and pepper to taste.

  3. Finish by incorporating sliced onions and simmering until all the ingredients are tender, creating the distinctive tangy and savory Sinigang soup. Adjust the seasoning as needed before serving over rice.
